Collaboration
The iGEM competition emphasizes the interaction between teams. Indeed, we have collaborated with multiple teams. Collaboration allowed not only to share ideas, but also to listen to others.
3. Florida State University (FSU) team
We had a meeting with FSU team. As a beginner, we strongly need to have a chance to listen other team’s experience. In the meeting, we discussed project concepts of each other. We found both projects can be complementary to each other. After that we received some advices for the competition and learned some concepts of applicable genetic elements.
2. KU Leuven team
We participated the iGEM’s Den held by KU Leuven team. In the event, we listened other teams’ project presentation. We also showed our project and received experts’ comments. From experts, we are advised to build a mathematical modelling of our genetic circuit and reorganize the explanation for the appropriateness of our project.
3. Korea HS team, KUAS team and Sogang team
To raise the public awareness of synthetic biology in Korea, we have prepared a debate competition for both high school students and undergraduates. For the success of the competition, we put efforts to introduce experts who would give a speech for educating synthetic biology to participants, and designed the whole program together. Especially, our team took a role for hosting the event. This collaboration was much meaningful since Korean teams prepared an event together for the first time.
