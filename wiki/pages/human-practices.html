It is important not to remain our project in a lab, but to bring out to the real world. We’ve investigated how we can apply our project concept into the real wastewater treatment infrastructure. Our project, an intrinsic biosensor in algae, was developed with the application of gene modification into the wastewater treatment in mind. Thus, our human practice activities aimed to seek realization possibilities of the genetically modified wastewater treatment. To identify the current situation, we first visited four wastewater treatment offices in Incheon: Gwaja, Unbok, Gongchon, and Namhyang. In addition, we had a meeting with prof. dr. ir Di Wu, who is a expert on the wastewater treatment, for application of our project.

We visited Gwaja office and interviewed with assistant manager, Daeseok Park. Gwaja office has the biggest scale of infrastructure in Incheon. 350kt per day of foul water from Seo-gu district flows into the office. As the Bupyung-Juan Industrial park is located nearby, we realized that its main concern is dealing with unauthorized discharged wastewater. Park told us that specific elements of heavy metal in the wastewater are very difficult to remove through chemical reagent. In addition, if the inflow rate is increased over the acceptance level by the rainfall for instance, they cannot help but releasing incompletely purified water. In case of disqualification of released water, the office has to pay a fine, and this occasion has been frequently happened. Indeed, the released water had been only qualified on one day during 15 days of February, 2021. There is clearly a need for the more effective treatment.

At Unbok office, manager Jongki Lee guided us facilities and had interview. The office has tackled the domestic sewage from the residential complex near the airport. In the interview, he emphasized the burden from the increasing cost of chemical reagents and filter. He said that the price is especially burdensome in these days, as the raw material becomes expensive. This could be a reason of the application of gene modification in the wastewater treatment.
